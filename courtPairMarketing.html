Monte Carlo pairing engine

Each round, CourtPair evaluates up to 2,000 random arrangements and scores every one across five weighted factors — then picks the best. Weights are fully adjustable so organizers can tune the balance between skill fairness, variety, and wait-time equity.

Core features
🎯
Smart court generation
Supports singles, doubles, and mixed-doubles formats. Players can be added mid-session at any time without disrupting ongoing rounds. Partial-round support lets finished courts start a new game while others are still in play.
📡
Live multi-device sync
Players join by scanning a QR code or entering a session code. Scores, rounds, and settings sync to other app users over any internet connection — WiFi or cellular — with no server required.
🏆
Standings & scorekeeping
Live standings by win percentage with point-differential tiebreaker. Score entry directly on each court card, with instant recalculation.
👥
Role-based access control
Assign Host, Admin, Scorer, or Player roles to connected devices. Each role has precisely scoped permissions — from score entry to full session control.
🏅
Tournament bracket mode
Three formats: single-elimination, double-elimination, and reseeded rounds. Automatic bracket advancement and rendering — no manual bracket management needed.
💾
Save, restore & resume
Export a full session snapshot — scores, rounds, standings — and restore it later to resume the competition on another day. Or import roster & settings only to carry returning players into a new competition.

Player management

  • Add or remove players at any time — even mid-session
  • Late arrivals join seamlessly; the algorithm picks them up next round
  • Gender field for mixed-doubles balancing
  • Initial skill ranking for seeded grouping
  • Sit-out toggle to exclude from future rounds
  • Wait-count tracking to prevent unfair skips
  • Push notification alerts when it's your turn to play

Organizer controls

  • Configurable court count and game format
  • Pairing mode: normal, grouped, or rotating
  • Ranking basis: current standings or initial rank
  • Per-factor weight sliders (0–10 scale)
  • Delete matches, rounds, or entire sessions
  • Printable scoresheets and standings reports
